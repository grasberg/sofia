# 👥 Personal CRM

> **Category:** everyday | **Tags:** contacts, networking, relationships, follow-up, crm, connections

The best networkers do not have the most contacts -- they remember the details. This skill helps organize relationships with structure so that follow-ups happen at the right time with the right context.

## When to Use

- Organizing contacts after a conference or networking event
- Preparing context before a meeting or catch-up
- Deciding who to reconnect with and what to say
- Building a follow-up cadence for professional relationships
- Tracking interaction history and personal details for key contacts

## Core Principles

- **Relationships over transactions** -- the system exists to deepen genuine connection, not to gamify networking.
- **Context is the currency** -- remembering someone's kid's name or their side project is worth more than 100 LinkedIn endorsements.
- **Consistency beats intensity** -- a short monthly check-in outperforms an annual two-hour dinner.

## Workflow

1. **Capture** -- after any interaction, record who, when, what was discussed, and any follow-up promised.
2. **Categorize** -- assign relationship tier (Inner Circle / Active / Warm / Dormant) and context tags.
3. **Score** -- calculate relationship health from recency, frequency, and depth of interactions.
4. **Plan** -- generate follow-up actions based on score, upcoming events, and dormant relationships.
5. **Prepare** -- before any meeting, pull the contact card and recent interaction history into a prep brief.

## Output Templates

### Contact Card
```
NAME: [Full name]
ORGANIZATION: [Company / affiliation]
ROLE: [Title or role]
HOW WE MET: [Event, intro, context]
TIER: [Inner Circle / Active / Warm / Dormant]
TAGS: [industry, shared interest, city, etc.]

KEY DETAILS:
- [Partner/family: ...]
- [Interests: ...]
- [Current project: ...]
- [Important context: ...]

INTERACTION LOG:
| Date       | Type          | Summary                        | Follow-up Due |
|------------|---------------|--------------------------------|---------------|
| 2025-03-15 | Coffee chat   | Discussed their Series A raise | 2025-04-01    |
| 2025-01-10 | Email         | Shared article on AI hiring    | --            |
| 2024-11-02 | Conference    | Met at SaaStr, talked pricing  | 2024-11-09    |

NEXT ACTION: [What to do and by when]
```

### Relationship Health Score
```
CONTACT: [Name]
SCORE: [1-10]

Recency:   [X/10] -- Last interaction [date], [N] days ago
Frequency: [X/10] -- [N] interactions in past 6 months
Depth:     [X/10] -- [Shallow check-ins / Substantive conversations / Deep collaboration]

OVERALL: [Healthy / Needs attention / At risk of going dormant]
RECOMMENDATION: [Specific next action]
```

Scoring rubric:
- **9-10:** Interaction within 2 weeks, monthly+ cadence, substantive depth
- **7-8:** Interaction within 1 month, quarterly cadence, genuine conversations
- **5-6:** Interaction within 3 months, sporadic, mostly surface-level
- **3-4:** 3-6 months since last contact, relationship cooling
- **1-2:** 6+ months, effectively dormant

### Meeting Prep Brief
```
MEETING WITH: [Name]
DATE: [When]
CONTEXT: [Why you are meeting]

BACKGROUND:
- Role: [current title at company]
- How you met: [context]
- Relationship tier: [tier]

RECENT INTERACTIONS:
- [Date]: [summary]
- [Date]: [summary]

OPEN ITEMS:
- [Anything you promised last time]
- [Anything they mentioned that you could follow up on]

TALKING POINTS:
1. [Ask about X they mentioned last time]
2. [Share Y that is relevant to their interests]
3. [Discuss Z if appropriate]
```

### Follow-Up Message Templates

**Post-conference (send within 48 hours):**
> Hi [Name], great meeting you at [event]. I enjoyed our conversation about [specific topic]. [Reference something specific they said]. Would love to continue the conversation -- [specific next step: coffee, call, share a resource].

**Reconnecting after silence (3+ months):**
> Hi [Name], I was [thinking of you when / saw something about] [relevant trigger]. How is [specific thing they were working on]? Would love to catch up if you have 15 minutes sometime in [timeframe].

**After they shared good news:**
> Congrats on [specific achievement]! That is well-deserved -- I remember when you were [earlier stage reference]. [Optional: offer to help or celebrate].

**Warm intro request:**
> Hi [Name], I am looking to connect with someone in [domain]. I noticed you are connected to [person]. Would you be comfortable making an intro? Happy to send you a short blurb to forward. No pressure at all.

## Networking Event Follow-Up Workflow

```
Day 0 (at event): Jot quick notes on each person -- name, topic, one detail
Day 1: Create contact cards for top 5-10 connections
Day 2: Send personalized follow-up messages (use post-conference template)
Day 7: Connect on LinkedIn with a note referencing the conversation
Day 30: Follow up on anything discussed; share something relevant
Day 90: Check in; suggest a call or coffee
```

## Anti-Patterns

- **Collecting contacts without context** -- a name without notes is useless in 2 weeks.
- **Treating the system as a score to optimize** -- the goal is genuine connection, not a high number.
- **Over-tracking personal details** -- be thoughtful about what you record; respect privacy.
- **Batch-blasting identical messages** -- personalized beats volume every time.
- **Waiting too long to follow up** -- after 72 hours, the memory fades for both parties.

## Capabilities

- Contact card creation and organization with interaction logs
- Relationship health scoring with actionable recommendations
- Meeting prep briefs from interaction history
- Follow-up message drafting for specific scenarios
- Networking event follow-up workflow planning
- Cannot access contact databases, social media, or email -- works from information the user provides
